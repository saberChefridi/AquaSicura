Philosophy

The Ancient Greeks

Thales of Miletus declared water the arche — the fundamental principle from which all things emerge. To the ancients, water was not merely a substance but the breath of creation itself.

Modern treatment strips water of its minerals and memory. What arrives at our taps has traveled through miles of pipes, losing its structure, its minerals, its very essence. We drink, but we are not nourished.
